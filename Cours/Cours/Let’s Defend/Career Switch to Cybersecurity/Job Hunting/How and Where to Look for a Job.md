# How and Where to Look for a Job?
Before we take a look at the job postings, we would like to mention how and where you can find these job postings.
### Job Search Platforms
We should definitely utilize the "job search platforms" to find job
postings in every sector possible. Since the blue team industry is a
recently growing industry, job postings for the blue team area may not
be available on all platforms, and employers generally prefer specific
platforms.
If you want to find blue team job postings, you should definitely take a look at the platforms below:
### LinkedIn.com
LinkedIn is a social media platform for professionals and it is a
popular social networking site that brings united all kinds of
professionals of different origins. It is among the major job search
platforms to look for new opportunities as well, especially for the IT
field thanks to the convenience it provides such as easy apply feature
and etc.
The most basic feature that distinguishes LinkedIn from other social media platforms is that it is business-oriented.
### Indeed.com
Indeed is another online platform that helps searching new
opportunities. You can filter down the job postings by entering the job
title and location which gives a lot of flexibility to the job
searchers. You can also access the reviews about the company on the
“Company reviews” page on Indeed.
### Keywords in Job Searches
There are tens of thousands of open positions and job postings
available on these online platforms within different sectors. We should
use some keywords in order to find the job postings that we want among
these postings and that we think are good fit for
SOC Analyst
Tier 1 Analyst
Tier 2 Analyst
Security Analyst
Incident Responder
Malware Analyst
Threat Hunter
### Organization's Own Career Pages
If you have a specific institution in mind that you want to work for,
you can check the company’s own career pages. For example, you can
reach the job posting page of IBM at the following link.
https://www.ibm.com/employment/
![](https://ld-images-2.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/Job+Hunting/images/IBM-career.png)
You can access companies own open positions within their sites under
categories like “Careers”, “Jobs”, and “Opportunities” from their
navigation menu or the menus at the bottom of the page.
### Establishing one-to-one contact with managers
You can send a private message to the SOC team leader/manager in the
companies you want to work with on platforms such as LinkedIn and ask if
there is a position that fits your skillset.
There is a possibility that this method is not always welcomed by the
organizations. While some hiring managers will get impressed by your
communication and see how determined you are to get the job some others
may not like it and show adverse effect.
