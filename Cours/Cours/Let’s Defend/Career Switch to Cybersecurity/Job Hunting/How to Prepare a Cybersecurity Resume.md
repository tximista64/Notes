# How to Prepare a Cybersecurity Resume
### What is a CV?
CV stands for "**C**urriculum **V**itae" which means "resume" in Latin.
Hundreds, if not thousands, apply to job postings from large
companies. Since there is not much opportunity to interview each of the
applicants for 15-20 minutes, organizations scrutinize and pre-evaluate
candidate resumes and create a smaller pool of applicants to interview.
Since resumes are a prerequisite for going to the interview step, it is
important that they are prepared correctly.
A resume not prepared correctly may cost you a lot, it may prevent
you from taking the next step. Let's see what we should pay attention to
when preparing a resume.
Share your resume in pdf format! Do not use other formats, such as doc, docx, etc.
### What information should you include in your resume?
We mentioned that the resume plays a key role for you to take the
next step in your job applications. So, should we give as much
information about yourself as possible in your resume and ensure that
the reviewer learns all the information about you? The answer is “No”.
Organizations receive hundreds of applications for the job postings
they share. It is not possible for them to have one-on-one interviews
with each candidate for 15-20 minutes, and it is not possible to read a
10-page resume from hundreds of candidates one by one. You should
prepare your resume as short as possible covering the maximum
information.
In order to minimize the number of pages, you should arrange the
information you want to include in your resume accordingly. The most
basic information that should be on your resume is as follows:
### Your Name
It is the information that you must input in order for the evaluator to know whose resume he/she is reviewing.
### Your Contact Information
Your contact information is required in order for the hiring manager
to reach you. Your resume must contain your email address and phone
number. Your CV may be highly appreciated by the hiring manager or the
reviewer, but if they are not able to reach out to you, your efforts may
go to waste.
### Your Education Information
Your educational background is crucial so that you can impress the
hiring manager to be selected among the candidates for the next step. We
recommend that you include the colleges that you attended for
bachelor's and graduate education indicating the majors you studied (you
don’t need to give out any further information). We recommend that you
include this information in your resume even though you didn’t study in
the same field/major as the job posting you applied for as some
companies still value the candidate's major more than his/her talent,
unfortunately.
### Work Experiences
This is the most important part from the hiring manager’s
perspective. The hiring manager will decide your compatibility for the
position you have applied for by reviewing your past work experiences.
You should include all the following information in detail for each of
the work experiences you have listed.
- Your responsibilities
- Your tasks
- The products/tools/software that you have hands-on experience
### Your Projects
It will be advantageous for you to include the projects you directly
worked on or contributed to in your resume. The projects you add to your
resume will show your interest in the field.
### Your Certificates
Certificates show how competent you are in your field and you should
definitely mention the certificates you currently have in detail in your
resume.
Although there are 2 different views towards certificates in the
Infosec world, it cannot be denied that it will be advantageous for you
in your job applications.
### Your Personal Blog/Website
Your personal website will be a good reference point about you both
for hiring managers and for the ones who would like to get in-depth
information about your background so you should mention your personal
blog or website if you have any on your resume.
### Your Foreign Language Proficiency Level
You should include your language skills in your resume, especially if
you are multilingual. Since English is considered the technology
language all over the world there are English language requirements for
cybersecurity positions in many countries where English is not the
spoken language. Being multilingual will help you in your investigations
and documentation other than the English language.
### Your Overseas Work Visa
If you are applying for a job abroad and you have a proper work visa, you should definitely indicate this in your resume.
### What to Avoid in Your Resume
Your resume should be as simple, short, and concise as possible. If
you include unnecessary information other than what we have mentioned
above it won’t make any impact on the hiring manager, it may actually
have the opposite effect.
You don’t need to mention the following information on your resume:
- Your pre-University/College education,
- Your photo,
- Your salary expectation,
- Your marital status
### Keywords to use in your resume
Large technology companies normally receive applications in the
exceeding amount when they open new positions. In such cases, there is
less possibility for your resume to be visible to the hiring manager and
we recommend using the keywords properly. Companies use different tools
to scan hundreds of resumes they receive in a digital environment to
avoid going through every single one of them. These tools look for
specific keywords indicated in the job description and eliminate the
resumes missing certain keywords. So creating your resume by using
certain keywords in a smart way will increase the chance of your
resume’s visibility.
For example, you should name any of your experiences in the work experiences section as "SOC Analyst" rather than "Analyst".
Following may be some keywords you can use in your resume:
- SOC
- SOC Analyst
- Tier 1 Analyst
- Tier 2 Analyst
- Incident Responder
- Incident Response
- Investigation
- Security Operation Center
- Specific names of the tools and software you have experience
